Strengthening Student Food Security at JIBC

A Proposal to Support and Expand JISU’s Pantry Program

Overview

(S)ituation: Developed in collaboration with the Justice Institute Students’ Union (JISU) as part of the Current Trends in Security & Disaster Management course, this group project addressed food insecurity among JIBC students. The Pantry program faced challenges including limited funding, staffing shortages, low student awareness, and restricted access to fresh food options.

(T)ask: The project aimed to strengthen and expand The Pantry by improving sustainability, increasing community partnerships, enhancing outreach and education efforts, and identifying strategies to improve access, quality, and awareness of food support services for students.

(A)ction: Collaborated with JISU through meetings and a needs assessment process. Conducted a community profile and literature review to analyze student food insecurity and identify best practices. Applied the SMART framework to develop a structured project proposal including implementation strategies, risk mitigation plans, evaluation methods, timelines, logic models, and educational materials.

(R)esult: Delivered a comprehensive project design and implementation plan with short- and long-term recommendations to support sustainable program growth. Proposed strategies included expanding partnerships with food banks and farmers’ markets, improving storage infrastructure, increasing outreach initiatives, and exploring mobile pantry services for distance students. Developed infographics, educational content, a student Q&A guide, and a logic model to support awareness, accessibility, and long-term program sustainability.
